# Journal Shortlist — EEG-LeJEPA

Target paper: *"Edge-Deployable EEG Foundation Models: Scaling Laws and
Cross-Dataset Transfer for Compact SIGReg-Pretrained Encoders."*

Constraints applied: **free to publish** (subscription/traditional route; no
mandatory APC) and weighted toward **acceptance odds + scope fit**. All
quartiles are 2024 Scimago (best category); all journals below are **hybrid**,
so the listed APC is an *optional* gold-OA fee only — the subscription route is
free. Verified May 2026.

## Key reframe

Quartile is a property of the *journal*, not of the contribution type. An
empirical / applied paper that takes an existing method (here LeJEPA + SIGReg)
into a new domain is not capped at Q3/Q4. On rigor (leakage-clean re-evaluation,
multi-seed, significance testing, full reproducibility) this work clears a Q1/Q2
bar; the limiting factor is reviewer novelty-expectations and the deliberately
narrow single-corpus scope, not the quartile ceiling. Notably, almost all of the
free-to-publish fits below are **Q1**, with Cognitive Neurodynamics the clean Q2.

## Caveats

- **IEEE page charges.** IEEE titles (JBHI, TNSRE) enforce page limits with
  *mandatory overlength charges* even on the free route. Stay within the limit
  or budget a few hundred dollars.
- **"Best quartile"** is the highest across a journal's Scimago categories; a
  journal can be Q1 in one category and Q2 in another (e.g. BSPC). Reviewers
  won't care, but cite the metric accurately.

## Recommended order

1. **Biomedical Signal Processing & Control** (Q1, free, friendliest to a
   rigorous-empirics paper) — submit here first. *(Cover letter retargeted to
   this venue.)*
2. **Cognitive Neurodynamics** (Q2, on-scope) — fallback.
3. **Journal of Neuroscience Methods** (Q3) — guaranteed-landing safety net,
   especially under the evaluation-protocol framing.
4. **Journal of Neural Engineering** (Q1) — reach, only with the empirical
   insights as the headline contribution.

## Framing point that affects odds everywhere

Lead the abstract and contributions with the **empirical lessons** — the SIGReg
"M must scale with embedding width" pitfall, the leakage-clean evaluation
protocol, and channel-padding > re-pretraining — rather than with "compact
model." That converts a reviewer's "just an application" reaction into "useful
empirical contribution."
