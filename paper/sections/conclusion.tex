\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented EEG-LeJEPA, a 2.86\,M-parameter SIGReg-pretrained EEG
encoder runnable on a single consumer GPU in 10 minutes. The model
achieves 68.6\,\% / 0.751 AUC on EEGMMIDB rest-vs-activity and 62.1\,\% /
0.690 AUC on left-vs-right motor imagery (encoder mean-pool features) via
linear probe under leave-one-subject-out evaluation with subjects~1--20
strictly held out of pretraining; both gains over a random-initialized
identical architecture are statistically significant ($+11.7$ and
$+10.9$\,pp; $q<0.002$). We characterized the compact-scale regime across six (data,
compute) operating points under a leakage-free split, observing a
best-source AUC plateau near 0.76 that a 2.4$\times$ larger model modestly
exceeds (so the compact model is an accuracy/size trade-off for edge
deployment, not a capacity-optimal point), validated the SIGReg
anti-collapse mechanism via an explicit
$\lambda$ ablation including the $\lambda{=}0$ collapse-to-exactly-chance
demonstration, established a counterintuitive cross-dataset transfer
finding (channel-padded $>$ channel-matched), and showed that the SSL$+$linear
probe ties supervised-from-scratch training of the same architecture on
left-vs-right MI ($-0.2$\,pp, n.s.) and significantly exceeds it on
rest-vs-activity ($+8.4$\,pp, $p<0.001$), with one frozen encoder serving
both downstream tasks. Together these results
argue that compact single-GPU SSL pretraining at the right scale produces
useful, edge-deployable EEG representations, complementing the
foundation-scale recipe of concurrent work~\cite{laya2026} along the
in-distribution single-corpus axis.
